Current Research Projects

Modelling of RRAM Devices

M.Tech. Project

Manganite based bipolar RRAM are attractive for non-stochastic switching, area-dependent current scaling, fast switching and long endurance, forming-less operation. Endeavors have been made to explain the current conduction and switching mechanism in these devices and various mechanisms have been proposed including interface resistance switching, SCLC and Metal – Insulator Mott-Transition. In this project, we have identified SCLC as the current conduction mechanism where current depends on traps created by oxygen vacancies in the device. An analysis of the evolution of oxygen vacancies with voltage is important to understand the current-voltage characteristics of such devices.

  • Completed Work

    We have developed a parameter extraction methodology based on single, uniform trap level in Pr0.7Ca0.3MnO (PCMO) based RRAM devices to extract energy distribution of traps from experimental data. Extracted parameters like mobility, trap depth using the developed model. Extracted trap concentration ( 1018/cc) at high(Trap-filled limit regime) and low(Ohmic regime) voltages, which exhibited good correlation. Demonstrated non-uniformity in trap distribution as the reason for observed asymmetry in I-V characteristics using TCAD simulations.

  • Current Work

    Currently, we have observed the significant contribution of an additional trap level in these devices under different process conditions. We are working on developing a two-trap model, based on the contribution of both deep traps and shallow traps to the I-V characteristics.

Analysis of Selector-less RRAM Devices

Associate Project

Resistive random access memories (RRAM) are promising due to its fast switching, endurance, retention, 4F2 cell size. However, the sneak path current issue in memory crossbar array results into reduced readout margin, and increased power consumptions which limits the maximum crossbar array size. Several selector devices are being proposed to address these problems. However, adding selector in series with RRAM increases the operating voltages and structure complexities. Hence, selector-less RRAM with non-linearity in LRS currents (ILRS) are being proposed. However, the non-linearity (NL) and memory window (MW) values of these devices are insufficient to realize a selector-less crossbar array.

  • We propose a PCMO based RRAM device with a record NL of >160, MW of >160 along with a slope of ~15 mV/dec in ILRS. A record sharp slope of ~16mV/dec was observed in ILRS of P650C sample.
  • The SCLC physics was used to obtain trap density and trap energy level.

SCLC and Band Bending in M-I-M Structures

M.Tech Seminar (Sep 2014 - Nov 2014)

Metal-Insulator-Metal(M-I-M) structures are a growing trend in resistive switching technologies like RRAM and many combinations like Pt/HfO2/TiN, Cu/Ta2O5/Cu and also HfxAl1-xOy systems. Electrostatics in these systems are usually governed by traps created by oxygen vacancies. In this project, we have studied the effect of traps on the potential profile and equivalent energy-band diagram.

  • Derived an expression for potential profile for known trap-free semiconductor/insulator at equilibrium by solving the 1-D Poisson equation for M-I-M structure
  • Derived an expression for potential profile for known a uniform, single-level trap profile at equilibrium by solving the 1-D Poisson equation for M-I-M structure
  • Verified the expression using TCAD simulations.
  • Formulated a methodology to extract trap density from Ohmic current density using this expression.

Undergraduate Projects

Co-operative Sensing in Cognitive Radio Networks

We propose a co-operative sensing framework, in conformation to the regulations stipulated by IEEE 802.22 WRAN standard. The objective of our work is to provide an ecient, yet, easily implementable spectrum discovery scheme following 802.22 standard. Through our work, we have shown that our proposed sensing framework achieves a lower probability of false detection and shows that the sensing procedure can be carried through less expensive receivers with lower sensitivity than those used in other traditional centralized sensing frameworks.
